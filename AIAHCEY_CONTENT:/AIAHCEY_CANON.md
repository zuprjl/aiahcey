# AIAHCEY_CANON.md

> Working document derived from recurring fandom observations, reactions, and language.
>
> This file does not attempt to define what Aiahcey objectively is.
>
> Instead, it documents how the fandom consistently experiences, describes, and reacts to Aiahcey.

---

# The Aiahcey Effect

The most common observation across the fandom is that Aiahcey does not require a major interaction to create an impact.

Fans frequently describe reacting to:

- standing beside each other
- walking together
- appearing in the same frame
- performing together
- being shown together on screen

The appeal is often described as a feeling rather than a specific event.

A recurring sentiment:

> They don't have to do anything.
>
> They just have to exist next to each other.

---

# Visual Chemistry

Fans repeatedly describe Aiahcey using phrases such as:

- visual chemistry

- on-screen chemistry
- bagay sila
- chemistry is insane

Many fans specifically reference:

- LED screen shots
- concert footage
- stage pairings
- performance moments
- walking clips
- side-by-side framing

The fandom often treats visual chemistry as evidence in itself.

# Femme × Femme

A defining aspect of fandom language.

Fans often describe Aiahcey as a uniquely feminine pairing.

The appeal is not rooted in contrast alone.

Instead, fans describe two feminine energies amplifying one another.

Recurring words include:

- kikay
- femme x femme
- elegant
- pretty girls
- visual pairing
- soft femininity

This theme appears frequently when fans discuss photos, performances, and casual interactions.

Fans often describe Aiahcey as having the ability to naturally match each other's energy.

- silent comfort

A recurring interpretation is that neither person dominates the dynamic.

# The Pull Effect

One of the most recognizable fandom experiences.

Fans frequently describe entering the fandom with a single bias and unexpectedly becoming attached to both.

Common reactions:

- "I came here for Aiah."
- "Why is Stacey suddenly here?"
- "I was supposed to focus on one person."
- "Now I have two problems."

The fandom often jokes that Aiahcey makes it difficult to focus on either individual member separately.

# Website Thesis

The goal of the website is not to prove that Aiahcey exists.

The goal is to document and explain why fans consistently react to Aiah and Stacey the way they do.

Every featured photo, tweet, video, and observation should answer:

> What part of the Aiahcey Effect does this demonstrate?

If a piece of content does not help answer that question, it likely does not belong in the core archive.

---

# Editorial Principle

Moments are evidence.

Patterns are the story.

The website should prioritize recurring themes, observations, and fandom interpretation over simple chronological documentation.

Aiahcey is not defined by a single interaction.

It is defined by the cumulative effect of many moments viewed through a shared fandom lens.
